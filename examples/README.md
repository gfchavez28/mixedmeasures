# Worked example

A complete, synthetic mixed-methods study you can open and take apart to learn
Mixed Measures end to end.

## Trailhead Math evaluation — Ferncrest School District (2024–25)

A fictional district evaluates a new inquiry-based elementary math curriculum,
"Trailhead Math," rolled out across eight schools. The study is **explanatory
sequential**: the assessment numbers show *which* schools improved, the teacher
focus groups and principal interviews explain *why*, and the two strands come
together on the integration Canvas.

It exercises the full workflow: importing survey/assessment data, recoding and
computing scales, running group comparisons, coding interviews and documents
against a shared codebook, linking participants across sources, and writing up
findings with live excerpts and results on the Canvas.

> Everything in the data — names, schools, the district, people, and the
> "Trailhead Math" curriculum — is invented for teaching purposes. Do not treat
> any figure as a real finding.

## Get the files

The sample data set is distributed with each release (it's data, not code, so it
lives with the installers rather than in the source tree):

- **`ferncrest-example.mmproject`** — the finished project. Open it directly in
  Mixed Measures to explore a completed study.
- **`ferncrest-example-raw.zip`** — the raw source files (four datasets, eight
  principal interviews, four teacher focus groups, two program documents) if you'd
  rather build the project yourself by following the walkthrough.

Download both from the [latest release](https://github.com/gfchavez28/mixedmeasures/releases/latest).

## Walk through it

[`ferncrest-walkthrough.md`](ferncrest-walkthrough.md) rebuilds the study from the
raw files, start to finish — about an hour or two at an unhurried pace. Building it
yourself is the fastest way to learn how the pieces fit together; opening the
finished `.mmproject` and taking it apart is the quicker tour.
